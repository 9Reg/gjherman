The premise

Memory has become infrastructure.

Valentine Ortiz built the algorithms that protect the government’s most valuable asset: combat skills downloaded from thousands of elite soldiers and stored as memory. No one knows he’s been testing them on himself—no encryption, no safeguards, no way to lock them back up. Edward Vance lives in the shadows. A hacker. A memory bootlegger. He sells the very experiences Valentine was hired to secure.

Then, their worlds are torn apart. Both men become fugitives—hunted not for what they’ve done, but for what they haven’t. Set up. Chosen as disposable pawns in a much larger game. A powerful tech magnate needs the technology as his escape hatch from a past that’s about to surface, planning to sell it to the highest bidder and bury a wartime atrocity for a life of luxury beyond extradition.

Now, Valentine and Vance—guided by a mysterious hacker whose motives are unknown—must survive long enough to expose the lie, protect the country they both love, and clear their names, so they can answer only for the crimes they actually committed.

Genre

Near-future speculative thriller

with post-cyberpunk, speculative science fiction elements and a strong character-driven core. It blends the moral questions of Black Mirror with the pacing of a thriller and the tone of a grounded tech-noir road story.

Audience

Who's It For?

It’s a fun buddy road-trip romp with great dialog, brotherly bonding, assassins who take trophies, memorable freaks who simultaneously break and reinforce cliches and a self-serving, brilliant, murderous antagonist I liked so much I’m giving him his own story, somewhere….

Core question

Is convenience always right?

Imagine a world where memories can be bought, sold and stolen. Does the commoditization of memory devalue effort, and in return humanity itself?

Sequence

Series Potential

While it stands alone, it is the first in a planned trilogy (the second installment is nearing completion) exploring the evolution of identity, AI self-awareness, and the commercialization of consciousness.

Triggers

Who can't read it?

There are no triggers worthy of warning, standard trophy-taking assasins notwithstanding. Contains no overt sexual themes and only light descriptions of violence (it's a thriller, after all).
