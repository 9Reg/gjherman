The premise

Memory has become infrastructure.

In a divided near future, soldiers can be programmed, experiences can be bought, and the most intimate parts of a person can be stored, edited, and sold by the people who own the machines.

Five unlikely lives collide with the machinery that has been quietly shaping them, forcing each of them to decide whether the self is something remembered, owned, or chosen under pressure.
