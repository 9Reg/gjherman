Shudder

A 78,000-word work of literary horror with elements of speculative thriller, blending systemic dread with Gothic undertones of loss, failed connection and inevitable consequence.

Shudder is complete, polished and stands on its own while setting the world for further novels. It is horror in the best sense of the word: non-pulpy, with a literary spine and concerned as much with the compromises people make to keep systems running as with the terror pressing against the edge of the page.

Bio

GJ Herman writes literary horror, near-future thrillers, scifi, historical and speculative fiction shaped by history, technology, class, memory and the quiet machinery of power.

His work moves between intimate human fracture and larger systems in collapse, always returning to the question of what people become when survival asks too much of them.

American-born, he lives and writes in Germany.
